Passwords

Password attacks are prevelant attacks in the current landscape.
These attacks include; brute force attacks, dictionary
attacks, rainbow table attack and credential stuffing.
A strong password is compromised of seven components.
1. Length: Passwords should be longer than 10 character.
2. Complexity: Should include a mix of upper and lower
case characters, numbers and special characters.
3. Avoiding Common Words: Do not use common words
such as nouns or phrases.
4. Unique Passwords: Do not use repeated passwords
from other accounts.
5. Avoiding Predictability: Stay away from patterns or sequences e.g. passwords123.
6. Regular password changes: Change your password every specified time period e.g. 3 months.
7. Use Randomness: Using random assortation of characters, numbers and special characters
