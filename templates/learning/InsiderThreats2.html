Weapons Against Insider Threats

Employee Training

Continuously training all authorized users on security policy (e.g., password hygiene, proper handling of sensitive data, reporting lost devices) and security awareness (e.g., how to recognize a phishing scam, how to properly route requests for system access or sensitive data) can help lower the risk of negligent insider threats.

Identity and Access Management

Identity and access management (IAM) focuses on managing user identities, authentication and access permissions, in a way that ensures the right users and devices can access the right reasons at the right time. A key IAM function for preventing insider attacks is identity lifecycle management. Limiting the permissions of a departing disgruntled employee or immediately decommissioning accounts of users who have left the company are examples of identity lifecycle management actions that can reduce the risk of insider threats.
